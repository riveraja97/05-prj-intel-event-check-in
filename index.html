Sustainability Throughout the Ages

Explore Intel's journey through time and join our Summit Check-In!

1968

Intel Founded

Robert Noyce and Gordon Moore rename NM Electronics to Intel Corporation, laying the foundation for decades of innovation.

1971

First Microprocessor

Intel debuts the 4004, the world's first commercial microprocessor, igniting the computing revolution.

1978

8086 Processor

Launch of the 8086 processor, establishing the x86 architecture that drives countless PCs and servers today.

1985

386 Processor

Intel introduces the 386 processor with 32-bit architecture, enabling high performance and multitasking PCs.

2006

Peak GHG Emissions

Intel reaches its highest greenhouse gas emissions; invests in renewable energy and efficiency to reverse the trend.

2020

RISE Strategy

Launches RISE (Responsible, Inclusive, Sustainable, Enabling) strategy with 2030 goals.

2022

Net-Zero By 2040

Intel commits to achieve net-zero greenhouse gas emissions across global operations by 2040.

2023

Renewable Electricity

Achieves 99% renewable electricity usage worldwide, reducing carbon emissions.

2024

Sustainability Summit

Intel hosts first Sustainability Summit, uniting suppliers, officials, and leaders to collaborate on sustainable semiconductor manufacturing.
